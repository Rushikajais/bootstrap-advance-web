Advance Computer and education

Computer education is the process of learning about computers and how to use them. It can be taught in schools, colleges, and universities, or through online courses and tutorials. Computer education can cover a wide range of topics, from basic computer skills to advanced programming concepts. The process of learning about and using computers is known as computer education. It may be taught in classrooms in schools, colleges, and universities as well as online through tutorials and courses. Basic computer abilities to complex programming ideas can all be covered in computer education. The purpose of computer education is to assist students in acquiring the abilities necessary for efficient and effective computer use.

Institutional Goals

1.Advanced computer education focuses on specific areas of computer science, such as programming, networking, and security. It also teaches students how to develop software applications and how to design and implement computer networks 2. The students will learn computer science and information systems application of critical, innovation and ethical decision making derived from computer science concepts, principles and theory in the resolution of computer information processing needs. 3. The students will identify and demonstrate basic knowledge and understanding of the application of state of the art computer science tools and techniques to meet personal and organizational needs through individual and cooperative group exploration and investigation. 4. To prepare students for graduate school in computer science, information systems, data science and MBA programs and/or to prepare students for professional computer industry certification programs. 5. To provide students that are not majoring in Computer Science with basic computer concepts and a knowledge of hardware, software, networks and security. 6.To provide a support structure to the classroom experience in terms of clubs, professional organizations, cooperative programs, independent study, honors research papers, academic exhibits, contests and seminars.
